There does seem to be a correlation between Health Risks and Age, Income

Below you will find analysis of of the different data sets from graph above:

Poverty vs. Healthcare, Obesity, Smoking

As poverty increases, there seems to be a lack of healthcare increases, obesity rates increase, and more money is spend on smoking.

Age vs. Healthcare, Obesity, Smoking

As age increases there is a trend towards decrease in healthcare and obesity, but increase in smoking. This dataset doesn't seem to include enough data to make a final recommendation, just that there does seem to be some correlation.

Income vs. Healthcare, Obesity, Smoking

As Income increases, both obesity and lack of healthcare decrease. There doesn't seem to be a correlation between income and smoking.
